Engineering

The Kiron Engineering curriculum aims to establish knowledge of scientific and technical fundamentals in different fields of engineering and introduces the analytical engineering approach to solving real-life problems. All engineering study tracks start with scientific fundamentals in subjects such as mathematics, physics and chemistry. These are followed by more technical foundations in subjects like mechanics, materials science, thermodynamics or electronics, based on the studentâ€™s choice between Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Chemical, Environmental or Industrial Engineering. Interdisciplinary skills in project management, quality management and law are also covered to different extents in the corresponding engineering curricula. 

Example: 

Name: Mechanics I

Workload: 6 CP

Description
The module gives an introduction to the basics of statics. The main focus is on the mathematical description of the performance of rigid structures carrying loads. 

Topics include:

  • Equilibrium of coplanar/spatial force systems 

  • Stable and unstable equilibrium

  • Statics of rigid bodies

  • Moments and internal forces in bars and beams

  • Truss systems

  • Surface friction

  • Reduction of spatial force systems

  • Principle of superposition

  • Principle of virtual work

Learning Outcomes
After successfully completing the module, students will be able to:

  • analyze arbitrary plane and spatial equilibrium systems based on the concept of forces and moments,

  • calculate internal forces and moments of linear structures,

  • calculate system parameters influenced by friction,

  • apply the principle of virtual displacements,

  • evaluate the stability of equilibrium positions.
